Hardware-supported floating-point arithmetic

As outlined above, execution of software-equivalent floating-point computations produced from source by different compilers on different hardware architectures may lead to small differences in outcome, non-negligible for a deterministic computer. Thus, for this and a constellation of related reasons, hardware-supported floating-point arithmetic seems to be prima facie unviable for deterministic computing.26 Control the compiler and runtime stack top to bottom. Store a hardware and compiler tag and simulate when not on that platform. Support only a single hardware for the lifetime of a ship. Dock floating-point results. Check consistency of results.
